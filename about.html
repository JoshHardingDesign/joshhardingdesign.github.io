Why Me?

A Deep Instinct of Care

I’ve always been driven by making people feel cared for. Whether through service, music, art, or design, I’ve found fulfillment in building things that make people feel welcome, understood, and supported. Product design became the natural extension of that instinct.

Relentless Curiosity

“I don’t know” has never felt like a stopping point to me. I’m driven to understand how things work, why they break, and what makes them better. Product design gives me the space to ask better questions and turn those answers into meaningful solutions.

I Don’t Fear Problems

I thrive in problem space. I want to understand why an issue exists, who it affects, and how to solve it the right way. Whether it’s a product challenge or a broken appliance at home, I’d rather deeply understand the problem and the system that surrounds it than apply a temporary fix.

What's important to me?

Community

I love building and being a part of communities. Whether it’s the book club I’ve shared with the same eight friends for over 10 years, or the weekly dinner party we started in college that’s still going strong, I believe people are meant to be together. Amazing things happen when they are.

Learning Everything

It’s both a blessing and a curse to find almost everything fascinating. The upside is I never run out of things to read, explore, or build. I’m happiest when I’m learning something new, and lately that curiosity has led me deeper and deeper into software development.

Putting Good Things Out There

At the risk of putting product design on a pedestal, I believe we have immense power and an even greater responsibility. The digital experiences we create shape people in ways both big and small. I want to make sure the things I put out into the world genuinely make it better.
